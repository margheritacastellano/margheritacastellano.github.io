## MT180 — Ma thèse en 180 secondes {#mt180}

In 2026 I took part in **MT180** (Ma thèse en 180 secondes), the French edition of the international "three-minute thesis" competition, presenting my PhD research to a general, non-specialist audience. I made it all the way from the local round at IP Paris to the Île-de-France regional final, and then to the **national final** in France.

### Press

I was interviewed for École Polytechnique's news site about my PhD research and my run in MT180. The article, ["Margherita Castellano, des bulles et des maths"](https://www.polytechnique.edu/actualites/margherita-castellano-des-bulles-et-des-maths), covers how surfactant molecules — like the ones in soap — affect surface tension and let bubbles form and stay stable, and how I study these fluid interactions through mathematical models based on differential equations (the same Cahn-Hilliard-Navier-Stokes system I work on for my [research](/research/#phd)).
